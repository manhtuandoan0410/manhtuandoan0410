Chassis & Skeleton

The exoskeleton uses a titanium/graphene composite—lightweight and high-strength—to bear loads and distribute forces created by the artificial muscles. Internal fiber-optic sensing monitors structural health in real-time.

Muscles / Actuation

Graphene-Enhanced Dielectric Elastomer Actuators (G-DEA) behave like biological muscle—large strain, fast response. Electrodes use Graphene/CNT composites to remain flexible and conductive under high voltage. Tendons are Graphene-CNT composite fiber for extreme tensile strength.

  • DEAs: compliant capacitors that expand/contract under voltage (high speed, high force). fileciteturn0file1
  • Electrodes and tendons: CNT/graphene hybrids for conductivity and fracture toughness. fileciteturn0file0

Power / BESS (Bio-Energetic Spine System)

The BESS distributes flexible solid-state battery cells along the spine and tail, integrates thermoelectric generators (TEGs) to recycle waste heat, and adds kinetic/solar harvesting. This design balances weight and provides redundancy if any one segment is damaged. fileciteturn0file2turn0file8
